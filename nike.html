overview

The Nike Sports Research Lab (NSRL) scientists have been using antiquated systems and tools to collect elite athlete data when they come to the lab. As technology advances, researchers need better ways of visualizing, managing, and organizing the large data sets. They needed digital tools to integrate with their current processes, gather data from the antiquated systems, and share that information on a unified platform. The long-term goal was to create a platform for Nike development, design, and research teams to work together to create better experiences for elite athletes and customers.

A partnership was created between the product innovation, design, manufacturing, and research teams at Nike, to identify new tools and services that enable the production of better software and services. As an Experience Designer embedded on a team of five amazing software developers, I collaborated with members throughout the Nike Explore Team (NXT) to drive towards process and technology improvements, utilizing a user-centric research process. I lead the design of four applications that would build up to create the Path of the Athlete experience. I lead meetings with key stakeholders, ensuring that our projects met each groups' objectives in a timely fashion. I executed the strategy and vision for various NSRL projects, as well as owned deliverables like conceptual models, personas, scenarios, user journeys/flows, wireframes, and interface designs.

The result were four integrated yet separate software solutions: Fourtrack, Valet, Area42, and Voltron. Valet is a check-in service for researchers. Fourtrack allows researchers to pull data from the physical lab equipment, upload them to the cloud. Area42 is essentially a dashboard, allowing researchers to manage, visualize, analyze, and export athlete data. Voltron, a tool specifically designed to assign scan results from the 3D Body lab and integrates with Fourtrack.
